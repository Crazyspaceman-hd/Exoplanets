WHAT

This page contains charts relating to the planets have been discovered orbiting other stars.
This bar graph breaks down the host stars by the number of planets that have been discovered orbiting them. While the majority is most definitely held by single planet systems at this point it is worth noting that over a hundred 2 planet systems. Also interesting is the fact that systems of up to 8 planets have been located equaling our home system for planets orbiting a star.
This scatter-plot comparing the radius of a planet to it's mass illustrates the immense variation of planets to be found throughout the galaxy. The units are both in relation to Jupiter the largest planet in our solar system. Original work in this field favored the discovery of very large and massive planets as they would have a more noticable effect on their host star. The large number of planets clustered near 0 mass prompted the creation of the next plot. Note that rows containing null or 0 in the mass or radius columns were removed for this plot.
This plot is a significantly narrowed version of the plot above, it contains only planet with a radius less than that of Jupiter and mass below .2 Jupiters. The plot shows that while there appeared to the line at 0 on the original plot when focused down there is just as much variation in planets smaller and less massive than Jupiter as ones that are larger and more massive.
